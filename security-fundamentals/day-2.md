## Protecting Personal Data
Personal data protection involves keeping information about your life safe, including your family, work, home, and personal activities.

## Offline Identity (Offline ID)
Offline identity refers to who you are outside the internet:
- Your real-world life
- Family and home
- Workplace and personal activities

## Online Identity (Online ID)
Online identity is how you present yourself on the internet.
Even if you do not use social media, once you use the internet, you already have an online identity.

## Choosing an Online ID
When creating an online identity:
- Do not use your full real name
- Do not use your phone number
- Avoid information that can expose your real identity

## Risks of Data Exposure
Attackers can access personal data through:
- Hospital records
- School records
- Workplace information

They can use this information to:
- Impersonate individuals
- Commit malicious acts
- Perform identity theft

## What Hackers Want
Technically, attackers aim to exploit:
- Personal identity
- Medical
- Who Tracks Our Data Online

## Internet Service Providers (ISP)
Internet Service Providers can track online activities such as:
- Websites visited
- Data usage

In some cases, ISPs may sell user data to advertisers for profit.

## Advertisers
Advertisers monitor:
- Online activities
- Shopping habits
- Search behavior

They use this information to send targeted advertisements to users.

## Search Engines
Search engines collect personal information such as:
- Name
- Age
- Location
- Phone number
- Search history

This data helps them personalize results and advertisements.

## Websites & Cookies
Websites store user data using cookies to:
- Track online activities
- Remember user preferences
- Analyze behavior

## Organizational Data
Organizations also collect and store user data, which must be protected to prevent misuse.

## Key Takeaway
Many entities track online data. Understanding how data is collected helps individuals take steps to protect their privacy.
